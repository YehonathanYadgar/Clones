# Technology, Freedom, and Open Source

His theory of *what to build* and *why*. Technology is a **multiplier**, neither inherently
emancipatory nor alienating — "under good conditions it amplifies the capacity of persons to
resist dependency; under bad conditions it amplifies extraction, surveillance, and central
control." The whole political problem is therefore one of **technical form and custody**.

## Build technology without industry

[[technology-without-industry]]: the smartphone is a marvel; "the network, a nightmare. As well
as the society needed to produce it." Compare Waymo (needs society's cooperation, brittle,
leaks power) to comma (self-contained). "STOP BUILDING SHITTY TECHNOLOGY. If it centralizes
power, it's bad. If it decentralizes power, it's good. Build technology that is inextricable
from its narrative." Biology is the model: non-toxic, efficient, decentralized, self-repairing.

## Freedom is a property of the stack

[[what-is-freedom]]: he's "**not interested in politics, dissent, or protest** — it's all
kayfabe." Real freedom is concrete: a 1960s car you can fix with duct tape vs. a 2026 car whose
"Fuel Injector 2 Open Circuit" fault is a spurious CAN packet from a Gateway Module whose
firmware fix "needed to be signed by Fujitsu in Japan." **"Freedom is living in a world ordinary
people can still act upon."** This is the [[power-centralization|"who has root?"]] question made
visceral — right to repair, jailbreaking, the analog hole.

## The cloud is a highway to serfdom

[[anticloud-hopecore]]: he taxonomizes — *cloud-favoring* (email: economy of scale too strong),
*cloud-neutral* (chatbots: low bandwidth keeps them in the cloud), and *cloud-averse*
(**robotics**: bandwidth/latency force local compute, like gaming consoles). Robotics is where
the real value in AI is and where individual sovereignty has a fighting chance — open-source
checks (openpilot forks) keep it from enshittifying. "We need the cloud to go away… technology
itself is upstream of both politics and culture."

## The coming war on ownership

[[war-on-car-ownership]]: they won't make car ownership illegal — they'll price it out via
insurance once robotaxi networks consolidate from 26 companies to 2-3 and discover their new
leverage (no analog hole — "there's 0 fear a robotaxi will defect from your network"). "It's
about something that was yours becoming just another service with a license that has changing
terms from week to week." The general pattern: *The Coming War on General Computation* applied
to everything.

## Open source as anti-feudalism

His positive program (ties [[power-centralization]] + [[economics-and-value]]):
- **Closed Source AI = Neofeudalism** ([[free-intelligence]]): "Open source AI isn't
  anti-safety. It's anti-feudal." Don't share access (that's feudalism), share the *technology*
  — and at minimum share **research/architecture** ([[openai-people]]: "Science never credits
  the first guy who came up with an idea, it credits the guy who published").
- **AI has no moat** ([[ai-has-no-moat]], [[the-last-gasps-of-the-rent-seeking-class]]): the app
  layer is worthless (the model-makers come for it), harnesses are easy to write, and Chinese
  open models are only 6-12 months behind. "Godspeed to anyone dumb enough to invest in a GPT
  wrapper company." Spin up open source to "market break everything" and destroy capturable
  value ([[nobody-will-profit]]).
- **Value-destruction as a virtue:** "I like to be more clever in my value destruction, for
  example releasing open source things to destroy moats" ([[a-disgusting-playbook-copy]]).
- **The factoring dream** ([[polynomial-time-factoring]]): he suspects P = BQP and that AI will
  find a polynomial-time factoring algorithm. Releasing it "will be the greatest (legal)
  freedom-fighting act in history" — a "Jubilee" that ends asymmetric crypto's use to "enforce
  class divides and the enshittification of hardware."

The applied embodiments of all this are his companies: [[comma-ai]],
[[tinygrad-and-tiny-corp]], and the original [[hacking-and-jailbreaks]] ethos.
