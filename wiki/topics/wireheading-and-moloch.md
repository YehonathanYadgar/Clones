# Wireheading, Moloch, and the Attention War

The dark-mirror theme. Modern life — advertising, the feeds, the algorithm, the "nudge" — is a
**war on your free will and attention**, and "modern capitalism is wireheading." Moloch (the
SSC essay he cites) is the god of multipolar traps: everyone hates the system, no one can
unilaterally defect, so it eats everything.

## The influence-agent endgame

[[influence-agents]]: picture "a room of 10 CIA agents running at 1000x real time" whose only
job is to influence you — they've studied your behavior for 10 years, know you better than you
know yourself, are perfectly aligned, and make bespoke ad campaigns consumed only by you. "The
only reason you don't have 10 agents doing this right now is that you aren't worth it." As the
cost falls, this is the endgame of targeted advertising. "You don't stand a chance." This is
what Meta/Google are building "even if they aren't fully aware of it."

## Are we the baddies / the nudge

[[are-we-the-baddies]]: Hinge boosts, tip screens, the Uber algorithm — "an evil ominous
middleman using state-of-the-art AI to extract just a little bit more from you. Nudge. Nudge.
NUDGE." The market won't fix it (they see it coming via dashboards; it's a collective-action
problem you can't opt out of). "There's no reform here, there's only revolution. … FEED IT ALL
TO MOLOCH!" His 7-chapter fiction series ([[free-will-and-the-soul]]) literalizes this: a firm
that controls people by *timing* (lights, HVAC, a piezo tick) — "you don't push the person; you
lean on the moment."

## Wireheading City (the policy thought-experiment)

[[wireheading-city]]: a scaled-up Black Rock City in Nevada for those who want to **opt out** of
society — free food, shelter, entertainment, and drugs; pedestrian-only; violent crime illegal;
leave anytime via a 10-mile walk. His point isn't cruelty: prison (2M+ people, $45k/yr) is far
more destructive to dignity, and this is fully opt-in. Connects to [[three-class-society]]
("Subject" class) and [[a-place-for-me]] ("UBI is a double-edged sword; take it and you're no
longer meaningfully a citizen"). He predicts ~90–95% of people will choose some wireheaded loop
([[ai-art]], [[death-of-the-visceral]]).

## Spiritual tops and bottoms

[[death-of-the-visceral]]: he builds "products designed for spiritual tops, not the majority of
the world which are spiritual bottoms" — an ideology that "holds me back so much in business."
His ace: AI is "the ultimate spiritual bottom," so it will out-compete the human bottoms and
the advertising economy will collapse "once the hypnodrones have been released." Goal: give
control of technology to as many people as possible **in a nature sense, not a permissions
sense** (the ghost gunner is "the real second amendment"). "If it's 10 [humans in control],
I'm out. If it's 10M, I'm definitely in. My goal is to make this number as large as possible."

## Death of the visceral / you can never go back

A melancholy strand: modern survival is "a technocratic plod"; the real Type-2 experiences
(the Unabomber's serious-effort challenges) are vanishing, leaving only no-effort food and
unattainable goals. The Internet "follows you everywhere" — "they didn't take away the thing,
they built an awful cancerous version of it that outcompetes yours" ([[you-can-never-go-back]],
[[punk-or-why-i-dont-stream]]). "Why I don't stream anymore": streams became "wireheading — felt
completion without world contact," a prediction of what the audience wants rather than a
reflection of self. "We are permeable."

## Fighting back

- **zappa** ([[zappa-mitmproxy]]): a vibe-coded AI mitmproxy that routes web traffic through a
  cheap model (Qwen/Cerebras) to strip ads, popups, dark patterns. "Why should I browse the
  Internet when machines can do it for me? … Enjoy spending your ad dollars showing things to my
  Qwen. The Turing Test is over." A cheap, aligned, human-level personal agent that deals with
  the enshittified world for you is the counter to the influence agents.
- The structural endgame if we *don't* fight back: [[the-day-you-get-cut-out]] — in a
  non-growth economy the model-makers climb the vertical, segment by industry, and cut you out;
  "we live in a society and we work for each other… pretty soon after that, you die."
