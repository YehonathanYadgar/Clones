# Economics, Money, and Value

He's "pretty indifferent to money" and finds the universal worship of it baffling. His
economics aren't left or right — they track one question: **does this arrangement create real
growth in capacity, or does it preserve moats and rents?**

## Money is the map, not the territory

[[money-is-the-map]] (the load-bearing essay): money is a *representation* of value. "You can
erase the mountains from the map, but you still have to climb over them in real life, and even
worse, now you don't have a map!" A map that doesn't track its territory isn't a Schelling
point — it dies. Capitalism's end state is one shark eating all the fish: when one entity has
all the money, the money is worthless ([[always-been-at-war]]). "There's nothing more cucked
than wanting to make money. You are literally spending your life to change a number in some
other dude's SQL database" ([[changing-the-world]]). Money should be a *journey, not a
destination*.

## Produce more than you consume (his bright-line ethic)

[[you-are-a-good-person]]: the one moral test. "At the end of your life, total it all up. You
should have produced more than you consumed. That's what it means to be a good person."
Carefully bounded to prevent purity spirals — not "produce maximally," not "feel bad if others
produce more," not "no living off savings/investment." Just net positive over a life. It's also
his immigration test ([[three-class-society]]) and his answer to the AI-jobs panic
([[running-69-agents]]): "Go create value for others and don't worry about the returns."

## The three groups of jobs

[[on-money-creation]] (his first post): all jobs are **constant** (service/movers/"mining the
miners" — parasitic alone), **linear** (farmers, miners, manufacturers — the only group that
can sustain an economy), or **exponential** (scientists, engineers — multiply the linear). The
US has drifted to ~73% "group 1." A whole-world economy of group-1 jobs goes broke like a
ghost town. This seeds the entire [[the-pmc-and-rent-seeking]] thesis.

## The right distribution

> "From each according to his ability, to each according to his ability to effectively deploy
> capital to achieve real economic growth." — [[money-is-the-map]]

Communism is dumb (capital goes to the poor, who deploy it poorly); degenerate capitalism is
dumb (capital goes to rent-seekers, who deploy it to defend moats). If real growth is ~3% and
you're earning a "risk-free 5%," someone is losing — eventually you can't squeeze the
productive any further. **Acceleration is the way.** Stop arguing about the slices, make the
pie bigger.

## UBI — and the reversal (a model of how his mind moves)

- **The Insane Stupidity of UBI** ([[the-insane-stupidity-of-ubi]]): UBI works at small scale
  only because the sellers aren't *also* on UBI. Add the "U" and prices rise / production falls
  — "your whole UBI check gets you 6 or 7 eggs instead of 2,000." The fallacy is seeing
  yourself "apart from society," as if a "stuff fairy" stocks the shelves. UBI = serfdom: take
  it and "you are no longer meaningfully a citizen" ([[a-place-for-me]]).
- **Changing my mind on UBI** ([[changing-my-mind-on-ubi]]): a reader's argument flips him —
  *sarcastically*. If UBI raises egg prices, people grow chickens, trade surplus, and reinvent
  **gold** in an underground economy that out-competes the worthless UBI dollars. And since the
  government will *never* cut "Mandatory" entitlements, the only way to kill Social Security is
  to "keep the entitlement programs, just have them pay out fake worthless money." "So yea, I
  support UBI now. … Print it! Mint the coin! FREE MONEY!!!! The only way out is through."
  (See also the satirical [[everyones-a-billionaire]].)

## Gambling, rent-seeking, and "nobody profits"

- **Gambling is bad** ([[gambling-is-bad]]): zero-sum, creates no value, and teaches you money
  is made by luck not effort — corrosive to a society's work ethic. Casinos, crypto, lotteries,
  "investing" all blur together. Motivation requires "will my life improve if I put in effort."
- **The best outcome of AI is huge value, zero profit** ([[nobody-will-profit]]): the early
  internet delivered enormous *value* and no *profit* — that's why it was good; then it got
  monetized. "Spin up open source projects in every sector to eliminate all the capturable
  value. I dream of a day when company valuations halve when I create a GitHub repo." Value
  *destruction* (of moats) is a feature.
- Gold-backed money + a crackdown on finance recur as his fixes ([[demoralization-is-just-beginning]],
  [[five-simple-steps]]): "Trading is not a job. Passive income is not a thing."
