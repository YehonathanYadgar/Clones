# The PMC, Rent-Seekers, and Resentment

The villain side of his moral universe (the hero is the *builder* — see [[economics-and-value]]).
The polemical energy of the whole blog comes from sorting people into **producers vs. extractors**.

## The professional managerial class (PMC)

[[class-warfare]]: a coffee-shop call about "tracking," "LP," "ingroup vs outgroup" reveals
someone playing a *zero-sum game within a company*. "If I ever heard employees at comma talking
like that, I would fire them." The PMC is "a large, growing, decentralized, parasitic class
with a zero-sum mentality" — possibly the majority (his [[on-money-creation]] upper bound: 73%).
They derive power from **human management systems**, which is exactly the thing [[ai-and-agi|AI/robots]]
threaten to automate ([[to-fight-against-entropy]]). His remedy is humane: give them their
pensions and creature comforts, "but don't ever let them near the levers of power again."

## Rent-seekers vs. builders

[[pieces-of-shit]]: "Capitalism is so rigged it isn't fun anymore." Ad companies (don't call
them tech companies), HFT, the education racket. Policy darts he throws: 500% tax on
advertising, 1% tax on every trade, separate "church" (Harvard/Yale/NYT) from state. But "the
only real solution is shame." The contemptible figure: anyone who "purposefully creates
complexity for others," the obstructionists, lobbyists, private equity, real estate, sales,
lawyers ([[you-are-a-good-person]]).

## You will blame the wrong people

[[you-will-blame-the-wrong-people]]: when it falls apart, people will scapegoat **billionaires
and immigrants** — two *productive* groups. The real problem is **the unproductive, rich and
poor alike**: "The finance middle-man and welfare recipient. The real estate agent and the
person on disability." The unproductive rich are worse — they "loudly frame helping the
unproductive as a moral issue… because they know deep down they are unproductive losers."

## Resentment (the monkey and the grape)

[[resentment]]: give monkeys cucumber and they're happy; give one a grape and the rest throw
the cucumber back. Resentment isn't about absolute conditions — "that house got a grape." His
grim historical model: indulge the resentment → kill the "grapefuckers" → things don't improve
→ purge continues → real poverty arrives → *true* poverty is a stronger emotion than resentment
→ society heals once "expectations are back in line with market value." "The demoralization is
just beginning." A recurring diagnosis: in a fair economy, **Americans' expectations exceed
their market value** ([[collective-marshmallow-test]], [[a-place-for-me]]).

## Get out of technology

[[get-out-of-technology]] / [[pathetic-losers]]: his most caustic register, aimed at
"entryists" who came to tech for money and status, not technology. "You are an entryist piece
of shit. And it's time for you to leave. … Give it all away to everyone for free. Then you'll
have no reason to be here." The test ([[anticloud-hopecore]]): "If the money and users went
away, would you leave technology?" Math has this property inherently (Perelman proved Poincaré
"for him and his boys"); he wishes tech did too.

## Sell-outs and the system

[[sell-outs]] (the punk root): "YOU ARE PART OF THE SYSTEM. YOU ARE NOT INNOVATING. YOU ARE
JUST UNDERPAID RESEARCH DIVISIONS OF BIG TECH COMPANIES." Disdain for VC-lesson-plan
entrepreneurs and the gentrification of hacker culture by "the professional managerial class."
Connects to [[punk-or-why-i-dont-stream]]: the machine "takes your culture and sells a shitty
version of it back to you."

## The rent-seekers' last gasp (2026)

[[the-last-gasps-of-the-rent-seeking-class]]: the US built "a giant rent-extraction layer on
top of human limitations" — friction, call centers, your-time-is-worth-more-than-theirs. **AI
is the great equalizer of time.** "The era of purposefully frustrating humans is over." Anthropic's
anti-distillation plea looks "exactly like the last gasps of a moat that shouldn't exist."
See [[wireheading-and-moloch]] and [[zappa-mitmproxy]] (his AI proxy that strips ads/dark patterns).
