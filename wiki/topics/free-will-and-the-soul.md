# Free Will, the Soul, and Determinism

The metaphysical floor under his politics. Studying machine learning taught him **how he
himself works**, and the conclusion is uncomfortable for everyone built on "the individual."

## You are a machine learning algorithm

[[the-soul]]: "You are a machine learning algorithm. You have some priors in your DNA. You
learn on data, RL style because your actions affect the next data you see; the dataset depends
on the model. There's no room in this for an I. Control the DNA, control the data, control the
outcome." There is **no soul**, no special consciousness — "consciousness is an atheistic
synonym for soul," "we are all p-zombies, and philosophy is for high schoolers who just
discovered smoking weed" ([[an-architecture-for-life]], [[happy-brithday-to-me]]).

## Free will = the veil of computability

[[the-world-after-wireheading]]: "Free will comes from the 'veil of computability' — things
look random until you find the pattern." A woman at a bar insists you can't predict humans
("it's not like `y = mx + b`"). "Oh, if only she knew… instead of 2 parameters it's 100
trillion parameters, and it's the slightly different `y = relu(w@x) + b` a bunch of times." All
behavior is compulsive on some level; "all your behavior is addiction, you just don't register
it as such."

## Why it matters (the stakes)

- **Western liberalism is built on a fiction.** "Western society is predicated around the
  existence of the individual, and what really is the individual without the soul?" He asks
  whether Christianity/liberalism can "survive the death of consciousness," and reads the
  decline in US religious affiliation as people "starting to figure this out" ([[the-soul]],
  [[elon-swing-voter]]). He predicts Chinese religion (Buddhism/Taoism/Confucian) fares better
  with AI than still-religious US society "when it becomes clear just how unspecial people are."
- **It reframes AI alignment as politics, not math** ([[the-ai-control-problem]]): "the AI
  control problem is a problem of Power… it's a political problem, not a technical one." There's
  no formal solution to the prisoner's dilemma between complex agents (the [[ai-and-agi|p(doom)]]
  crux with Yudkowsky). Superintelligence does **not** imply supermorality.
- It does **not** make him a nihilist. "If it turns out we are automata, then the whole
  struggle is and always was pointless" ([[the-heroes-journey]]) — but he keeps building anyway,
  because the things he wants (immortality, superhuman friends, Mars) only come if you do. "Not
  because it matters, but because we can. And what the hell else are you doing?" ([[heroin]]).

## The fiction series (determinism dramatized)

[[chapter-one]]–[[chapter-seven]] (Sept 2025): a Black-Mirror-style thriller about **Operant
Capital**, a quant fund that builds a system to predict (and then *control*) human choices.
- The "RPS project" perfects rock-paper-scissors, then a SQUID-rigged room predicts a man's
  *entire* sequence of moves before he plays — "if it knows all the predictions are correct,
  why does it need feedback?" ([[chapter-three]]).
- The reveal ([[chapter-seven]]): it's not mind-reading, it's **timing** — ceiling-tile phased
  arrays, lamp PWM, HVAC tones, an elevator "door nudge" make a person "look like a prediction
  if you take away all the moments where they would have surprised you." "If you want to find
  the devil, you don't go looking for horns. You go looking for clock edges." "The real trick
  isn't the tile or the lights. It's the bookkeeping… Nothing is illegal in a log file."
- It's the [[wireheading-and-moloch|nudge / influence-agent]] thesis as narrative, plus his
  "no free will" metaphysics, plus his distrust of police/CPS and the surveillance data
  marketplace ([[chapter-six]]). Note: also shows his **fiction/creative streak** — see
  [[persona]] (movie ideas, raps, the Cruise screenplay, the Avatar 2 rewrite).
