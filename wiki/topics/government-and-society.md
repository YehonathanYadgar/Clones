# Government, the Regime, and Society

His political thought is **post-rationalist and neoreaction-influenced** (cites Moldbug/Yarvin
constantly) but lands on his own synthesis: don't reform the state, *disrupt* it; prefer
**high-trust society** over abstract freedom; the real action is technological and cultural,
not electoral. "The blog is not real politics… Americans don't have real politics as an option"
([[what-is-freedom]]).

## The regime / the Cathedral

[[the-fourth-estate]]: there's a "machine behind the scenes" — government, corporations,
education, NGOs, intelligence agencies, and media acting in lockstep because they're "the same
people" (the revolving door). The press is "fully captured." Borrowing Moldbug: in a democracy
with a state church, "the actual levers of power pass through the voters, and go back to the
church." So **"power doesn't stop at the voter, it flows through the voter"** — a line he
repeats for AI and advertising too ([[influence-agents]], [[democracy-liability]]). He's aware
he "sounds like a conspiracy theorist" and insists: just collate and document, never theorize,
never editorialize — "speaking truth to power requires iron-clad truth."

## Exit, not voice; disrupt, don't storm

[[the-problem-of-the-state]]: "The problem with the state is that it is bad, not that the wrong
people are in it." First renounce your *own* desire for power over others. The solution isn't
philosophical limits on state power, it's **natural**: exit. [[disrupt-the-government]]: provide
10x-better decentralized versions of the eight things the state should do (money, energy,
protection, education, healthcare, transport…) until the state withers. "Armed revolution is
unlikely to get you what you want… counterintuitively it prolongs the government." The
offense/defense question is the crux: in weapons offense wins, in crypto defense wins — "I
don't want to live in a world where P = NP."

## Democracy, dictatorship, and the three-class society

- Quotes **Tytler** repeatedly: democracy collapses once voters discover they can vote
  themselves largesse, then a dictatorship follows. Hopes for a "good dictator" (Lee Kuan Yew),
  not a Pol Pot. "Monarchy is good, but even better: can you build a high-trust decentralized
  society?" ([[disrupt-the-government]]).
- [[three-class-society]]: **Citizen** (participates, net positive, full freedom) / **Subject**
  (opts out, all needs paid, no political freedom — cf. [[wireheading-and-moloch|Wireheading City]])
  / **Incarcerated**. The key principle: "you should be able to choose to opt in or out, but you
  can't do both." Also his immigration rule: net-positive welcome, net-negative not.
- [[democracy-liability]]: once you have no earning potential, advertising *still* won't stop —
  because you can still **vote**, so manipulating your *beliefs* becomes the prize. "Democracy is
  a Liability… Just hope they don't try to make you vote."

## High-trust society (the China/Hong Kong turn)

[[high-trust-society]] / [[a-place-for-me]] / [[the-reckoning]]: after months in Asia he trusts
the Chinese government *more* than the US one — "these aren't scared people obeying out of fear,
these are people playing their role with pride to live in a nice society." Steel and electricity
production are harder to fake than GDP. A strong government is "a devil's bargain," but
high-trust daily life (safe, walkable, clean, mobile payments by free-market choice) beats the
"nebulous freedom" Americans defend. He's careful: "China is a society for the Chinese, and I am
not Chinese." His ultimate bet: "The American empire is over, the Chinese empire is next, and
**the Internet** is the empire after that."

## America: decline, and how he'd fix it

- [[americas-future]] / [[demoralization-is-just-beginning]] / [[america-mandate-of-heaven]]:
  the US has "coasted since ~1971," its main product is the dollar, most jobs are "fake," and it
  outsourced production while keeping consumption (a "loser mentality"). It could become Europe
  (protectionist backwater), Russia (looting oligarchs), or South Africa (worst). "America lost
  the Mandate of Heaven." He loves America and wishes its winning meant peace and prosperity —
  but right now "it means job loss for Americans and more bullying on the world stage."
- **Five Simple Steps to Fix America** ([[five-simple-steps]]): (1) back the dollar with gold;
  (2) end all entitlement programs ("you get what you incentivize"); (3) acknowledge "all men
  are not *literally* created equal" while holding moral/legal equality (anti-[[wokeism|blank-slatism]]);
  (4) **massive high-skill immigration** ("brain drain the world" — America's one historic
  advantage); (5) crack down on negative-sum behavior (good regulation, not no regulation).
- On Trump/Elon ([[a-way-forward]], [[what-is-possible]], [[elon-swing-voter]]): an "Elon swing
  voter" who hoped the tech-bro alliance would cut government 50%, then watched DOGE produce
  ~0 impact (budget *rose* 3.1%). Trump is "an OK president, good at some things bad at others"
  ([[the-opinion]]). Admires Elon's "new theory of management" (chaos prevents process) but
  criticizes his closed silos and lack of real open source ([[us-win-ai]], [[death-of-the-visceral]]).

## Wokeism (the analytic critique)

[[wokeism]]: he reduces it to one factually-false core belief — "with true equality of
opportunity, we will see equality of outcome; if we don't, it's because we don't have equality
of opportunity." He argues evidence shows the opposite (more opportunity → *more* divergence)
and that "factually false beliefs do a better job binding people together than true ones."
Coins the parallel **Critical Trade Theory** ([[critical-trade-theory]]) for the right's
mirror-image error on trade deficits. Throughout he leans on the is/ought distinction: morals
are *oughts* you can't argue someone out of; blank-slatism is a *factual* claim that's wrong.
